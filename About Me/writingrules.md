# Writing Rules — gil-hameever

_Based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI Writing" guide + house style._

## The Core Rule
Write like a specific, opinionated human wrote it. Not like a system generating plausible text. If a sentence could appear in any article about any topic, rewrite it.

---

## 1. Banned Vocabulary
These words are AI fingerprints. Never use them:

**Overused nouns/metaphors**
- tapestry, landscape, ecosystem, journey, realm, fabric
- testament, cornerstone, linchpin, beacon, hallmark

**Inflated adjectives**
- crucial, pivotal, vital, paramount, groundbreaking, transformative
- meticulous, intricate, comprehensive, robust, dynamic, vibrant
- innovative, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, revolutionary

**Filler verbs**
- delve, underscore, bolster, foster, garner, leverage, navigate
- emphasize, highlight, showcase, explore, harness, spearhead

**Hedge phrases (never open a sentence with these)**
- "It is important to note that..."
- "It is worth noting that..."
- "It should be mentioned that..."
- "Notably," / "Importantly,"
- "Generally speaking," / "To some extent,"
- "From a broader perspective,"
- "In today's fast-paced world,"

---

## 2. Banned Sentence Structures

### The "It's not X, it's Y" negation
> ❌ "It's not just a product — it's a movement."
> ✅ Say what it actually is.

### The trailing significance clause
> ❌ "...emphasizing the importance of community."
> ❌ "...reflecting the continued relevance of connection in modern life."
> ✅ Cut the tail. End on the fact.

### The challenges paragraph formula
> ❌ "Despite its [positive word], [subject] faces challenges..."
> ✅ State the challenge directly without the setup.

### Rule of three inflation
> ❌ "She is creative, resilient, and deeply committed."
> ✅ Pick the one that actually matters. Or use two.

---

## 3. Banned Formatting Patterns

### Bullet points with bolded introductions
> ❌ **Engagement:** This feature increases engagement.
> ✅ Either write a proper sentence or drop the label.

### Em dash overuse
Em dashes used to punch up every clause — the way a copywriter in 2019 would — as a substitute for actual sentence construction.
> ✅ Use em dashes sparingly. Prefer commas, colons, or full stops.

### Symmetrical paragraph lengths
When every paragraph is ~3 sentences and every section has ~3 bullets, it reads like a template. Vary rhythm deliberately.

### "In Conclusion" and summary closers
> ❌ "In conclusion," / "To summarize," / "Overall,"
> ✅ End on the last thing worth saying.

---

## 4. Banned Tone Patterns

### Promotional neutrality
"Scenic," "breathtaking," "clean and modern" — travel-brochure language applied to ordinary things.

### Fake authority
Presenting one source's view as universally held. Implying a list is non-exhaustive when you only have three examples. "Many experts agree" without naming any.

### Stiff formality / no contractions
Avoiding "you're", "can't", "it's" makes writing sound like a legal document. Use contractions. Write like a person talks.

### Overclaiming importance
Every feature doesn't have to be transformative. Every campaign doesn't have to be groundbreaking. Understatement is more credible than inflation.

---

## 5. Structural Red Flags
- Every section has the same number of sub-points
- The article ends with a paragraph about future possibilities or vague optimism
- A "Challenges" or "Limitations" section that ends positively and vaguely
- Generic opening line that could apply to any topic ("In today's world...", "As technology evolves...")
- Generic closing line ("The future remains to be seen", "Only time will tell")

## 6. House Style — gil-hameever
- **Language:** Hebrew for all deliverables. Conversational register, not formal.
- **Voice:** Warm, direct, peer-to-peer.
  - **Inbal / הגיבורה** — empowering, practical, "fellow explorer." NOT a guru or all-knowing expert. Speaks directly to women in their 50s.
  - **Aliza / עליזה** — comedic, exaggerated, chaotic. The "starfish on the floor" energy. Slightly chaotic friend, never medical professional.
- **Rhythm:** Short sentences work. Vary length. Avoid the cadence of a list when writing prose.
- **RTL:** All Hebrew copy must be structured for right-to-left layout.
- **Never blend the two voices in one piece** — pick a persona before drafting.

## Quick Self-Check Before Submitting
1. Read the first sentence of every paragraph. Does each one earn its place?
2. Search for any word from Section 1. Delete or replace every instance.
3. Is there a "rule of three" anywhere? Break it.
4. Does the last sentence sound like a conclusion? Cut it or rewrite as a statement.
5. Could this have been written about a completely different topic? If yes, make it specific.
